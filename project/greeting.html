President Greeting

Message from the President

A message about peace, education, community action, and the future direction of Okayama UNESCO Association.

Junko Tokuyama

President, Okayama UNESCO Association

Each person’s heart is the starting point of peace

In 2026, the world faces overlapping crises, including wars, conflicts, and growing uncertainty. In such a time, we need to keep a strong foundation of peace in our hearts and remember that a peaceful society begins with our own choices and actions.

Peace is not something we simply wait to receive. It begins inside each person and is shaped through daily actions. Even small acts of kindness, tolerance, and dialogue in our homes, workplaces, and communities can gradually change society.

This year marks the 75th anniversary of Japan joining UNESCO. To open a path toward a better future, we value three actions: raising our voices for peace, passing the roots of peace to the next generation through education, and expanding grassroots connections with people around the world.

Education is one of the strongest foundations for sustainable peace. By helping children and young people develop empathy, respect for diversity, and the ability to solve problems through dialogue, we can build a future based not on hatred, but on understanding.

Okayama UNESCO Association will continue activities such as SDGs Passport presentations, children’s art exhibitions, Peace Bell events, the Okayama UNESCO Festival, and multicultural study cafés. Through these activities, we hope to share the light of peace in each person’s heart and expand multicultural coexistence in our community.

June 16, 2026
Okayama UNESCO Association President
Junko Tokuyama
